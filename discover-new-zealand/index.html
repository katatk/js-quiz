About New Zealand

New Zealand is made up of 3 main islands - The North Island, South Island and Stewart Island. It has a relatively small population for its size, 4.47 million residents (according to the 2013 consensus), and only 17 people per square kilometre. Singapore by comparison has 8,000 people per square km!

Due to the low population density and the conservation work of DOC there are many parts of New Zealand that are unspoilt. There is an abundance of natural beauty to be found in our lakes, rivers, forests, mountains and coast.

The mild climate has allowed many plant and animal species to flourish here, they have evolved largely in isolation from predators, making them vulnerable to introduced pests and diseases. This is one of the reasons New Zealand has such strict quarantine laws.
